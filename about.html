HISTORY
Wazalendo Art Project is a national organization with its base in Lunga Lunga, Nairobi KE. Its history began in September 2000 when Esapari and Vitendo Youth Group saw potential to develop an implementation of setting up an organization to awaken and uplift fellow brothers and sisters in this side of the equator through creative arts and crafts. We started off as a center where people(youths) would meet and do some crafting and drawing to salvage them from the deadly pangs of crime brought about by idleness in the Ghetto. Since then, Wazalendo has now grown to a national organization with branches in 26 out of 47 counties. The mere art activities that we engaged in to deter our minds from crime are now our source of income and a stable economic pillar in our lives. We depend on art for a living and can afford a posh life of our own choice. Our vision is to spread our wings across Africa by 2035 and eventually the whole world. Currently, Wazalendo is an established trademark in Kenya and is widely recognized as an elephant in matters Arts & Crafts due to its unique and quality products. We have an alumnus that stretches up to 5000 people since our inception. For instance, John Marube won The Kenya Art FAIR award in 2009 and was once a nominee in The First Annual Awards, a competition that is inclusive of all artists across the African continent.

ABOUT US
Wazalendo Art Project and the Government through the Ministry of Sports Culture & Arts initiated a programme called 'Jenga Yutman Initiative of Hope' which invites local artists and crafters to showcase their handicraft (arts and crafts) at the Wazalendo Art premises. According to Sikote Esapari, The CEO, the initiative will offer many artists the opportunity to have their craft exposed in the art market. This initiative is also designed to facilitate empowerment and community development. The Jenga Yutman Initiative of Hope aims to assist local crafters who are in need of space, training, marketing, advertising and exposure to their target markets. There's so much talent in Lunga Lunga. We must not shy away from exposing their talents as this may open many doors for them. We are planning to have a number of initiatives to grow the centre and make it more attractive to the public. However, the most important thing is that people visit the centre and ask us to assist them. This is a public facility and everyone is welcome here. We are here to assist the public in any way possible. Members of the public such as unemployed people, students, youth and entrepreneurs can make use of internet access and computers at the Wazalendo Art premises. Crafters will be given assistance and support through the following initiatives: Work space for artist and crafters Space and equipment will be provided at the Centre for people interested in sewing/weaving and beadmaking. Marketing opportunities Products will be showcased and marketed to local and international tourists visiting the centre. Selling of products No cash sales will be undertaken. Sales will only be made on order. A business card giving a selling price and a short profile of the artist or crafter will be attached to the products, which will be displayed in a glass cabinet. Training Training will be available to handcrafters and artists on a regular basis to ensure quality products and sustainable income for them.
